Adex Group

Mezzanine floors & warehouse space optimisation: B2B, organic only

Brighta Group · May 2023 – October 2025 · Organic search owned end to end; paid handled by a separate team

Adex Group (adexgroup.com.au) sells mezzanine floor and warehouse space solutions to contractors, procurement officers, and facilities managers, a considered, quote-driven buying journey close to Matic's. I ran the full organic engagement: audit, keyword and content strategy, and technical fixes.

What I owned

  • Full site audit, competitor keyword research, and content gap analysis to find ranking opportunities
  • Technical fixes: uncrawlable pages, a 404, a broken internal link, and an HTTPS-to-HTTP redirect
  • Restructured category and solution pages to address a high bounce rate and unclear navigation
  • Content strategy across 7 monthly phases, targeting commercial, long-tail terms like "mezzanine floor racking system" and "how much does it cost to build a mezzanine floor"
$7.7K AUD/mo retainer managed
~11,000 words of new/optimised content

SharkNinja: Shark Clean & Ninja Kitchen

Product e-commerce, Australia: organic SEO & AEO

WebProfits · July 2025 – present · sharkclean.com.au

SharkNinja is the global consumer appliance company behind Shark (vacuums and home cleaning appliances) and Ninja (kitchen appliances: air fryers, blenders, cookers). sharkclean.com.au is their direct-to-consumer e-commerce storefront for the Australian market, high-volume, high-SKU-count product pages where category and product-page SEO drive purchases directly. I own the organic SEO and AEO content strategy for existing and upcoming products: rebuilding page outlines, researching the questions buyers and LLMs ask about each product and brand, and preparing new product pages ahead of launch with the product team.

The Beauty Chef

Wellness & skincare-supplement e-commerce: organic SEO & CRO

WebProfits · July 2025 – present · thebeautychef.com

The Beauty Chef is an Australian wellness brand selling gut-health powders, collagen blends, and beauty tonics direct-to-consumer. I own the organic content and on-page strategy across the highest-traffic category and product pages, and worked on the content and UX copy behind the product-match quiz to qualify visitors into the right product earlier in the journey, alongside monitoring emerging channels like AI-assistant referral traffic.

Every funnel stage grew, but purchases grew fastest, up 90.4% against a 20.1% lift in landed sessions: the funnel didn't just get bigger, it got tighter. Cart-to-purchase (buyer basis) improved from 43.4% to 50.8%. The product-match quiz nearly doubled its reach (1.45% to 2.89% of landed visitors) at a steady completion rate, meaning more visitors are trying it, not just finishing it more often.
